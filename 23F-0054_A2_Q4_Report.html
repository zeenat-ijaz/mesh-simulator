1. Executive Summary

This report documents the implementation of a 2-stage circular q-shift on a toroidal mesh topology. The primary objective is to prove that utilizing the 2D grid structure significantly minimizes communication overhead compared to a basic 1D ring structure.

2. Theoretical Foundation

To perform a shift of q positions across N total nodes (where N is a perfect square), the mesh enables parallel routing across rows, then columns.

Mesh Complexity: (q MOD √N) + ⌊q / √N⌋ steps.
Ring Complexity: MIN(q, N - q) steps.

3. Demonstration (N=16, q=5)

Given 16 nodes arranged in a 4x4 matrix, shifting by 5 requires:

  • Row movement: 5 mod 4 = 1 step
  • Column movement: 5 / 4 = 1 step down

Total time: 2 steps. A ring would require 5 steps, proving a 60% improvement in routing efficiency.
